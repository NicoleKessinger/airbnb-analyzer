Finance Glossary

Every term and formula used in this analyzer — defined plainly, with the exact calculation shown.

✓ All formulas verified against the live app code · MBA 6223 Finance
🏦
Financing & Initial Investment
How the purchase is funded and what capital the investor actually deploys
Purchase Price
The agreed-upon sale price of the property. Every other calculation in this analyzer flows from this single number — it sets the loan size, estimated taxes, cap rate denominator, and (for all-cash purchases) the initial investment.
In this app: entered in Step 1. Required field — the analyzer cannot run without it.
Down Payment
The portion of the purchase price the buyer pays upfront in cash. The remaining balance becomes the mortgage loan principal. A larger down payment means less debt, lower monthly payments, but more capital deployed.
Formula
Down Payment ($) = Purchase Price × Down Payment (%)
Down Payment (%) = investor-chosen percentage, typically 20%
In this app: entered in Step 2. The % field auto-calculates the $ amount — they stay in sync. Defaults to 20%.
Loan Principal
The amount borrowed from the lender — the portion of the purchase price not covered by the down payment. This is the starting balance of the mortgage that will be repaid over the loan term with interest.
Formula
Principal = Purchase Price − Down Payment
Principal = 0  (for all-cash purchases)
In this app: principal = allCash ? 0 : price − down
Closing Costs
One-time transaction costs paid at closing, separate from the down payment. Typically include loan origination fees, title insurance, appraisal, escrow, and recording fees — usually 2–5% of the purchase price.
In this app: entered in Step 1. Added to the down payment (or purchase price for all-cash) to form the Initial Investment. They are not included in monthly operating expenses.
Initial Investment Capital Deployed
The total cash the investor must commit on day one to acquire the property. This is the basis for NPV and Cash-on-Cash Return — it represents the true equity at risk. Critically, it varies depending on whether the purchase is financed or all-cash.
Formula — Two Cases
Financed: Initial Investment = Down Payment + Closing Costs
All-cash: Initial Investment = Purchase Price + Closing Costs
For financed deals, only the equity portion (down payment) is the investor's capital — the bank provides the rest. Closing costs are always out-of-pocket.
In this app: initialInvestment = (allCash ? price : down) + closingCosts — used as the denominator in NPV and Cash-on-Cash. Never included in monthly expense calculations.
Monthly Mortgage Payment
The fixed monthly payment that fully repays both principal and interest over the loan term. Derived from the standard mortgage amortization formula — early payments are mostly interest, later payments are mostly principal.
Formula — Standard Mortgage Amortization
M = P × [ r(1 + r)n ] ÷ [ (1 + r)n − 1 ]
M = monthly payment P = loan principal (purchase price − down payment) r = monthly interest rate = annual rate ÷ 12 n = total number of payments = loan term (years) × 12
In this app: calcMortgage(principal, annualRate, years) — called for each financed scenario. Returns 0 for all-cash purchases.
💰
Revenue
Income generated by renting the property as a short-term rental
Nightly Rate
The price charged to guests per night. The single most powerful lever for revenue — a $25/night increase on 200 rented nights is $5,000 of additional annual income. Market rates vary by city, season, bedroom count, and listing quality.
In this app: fetched via live AI web search for your city and bedroom count. Shown as three scenarios (conservative / market / optimistic). Can be overridden manually in Step 4.
Occupancy Rate
The percentage of available nights in a year that the property is actually booked. A 65% occupancy means the property is rented roughly 237 nights per year. This is the second most important revenue driver after nightly rate.
In this app: each market-rate card includes a matching occupancy estimate. Typical Airbnb occupancy runs 60–80%. Can be overridden manually. Default if unset: mid-market estimate (~65–70%).
Nights Rented per Month
The number of nights the property is occupied each month, derived from the annual occupancy rate. Rounding at the monthly level — rather than the annual level — ensures the nightly rate displayed in the UI multiplied by nights displayed equals the revenue displayed exactly.
Formula
Nights/Month = round( 365 × Occupancy Rate ÷ 12 )
Annual Nights = Nights/Month × 12
Occupancy Rate = decimal (e.g. 0.68 for 68%) Annual nights is derived from the rounded monthly figure to prevent rounding discrepancies.
In this app: nightsPerMonth = Math.round(365 × occupancy / 12); then daysRented = nightsPerMonth × 12
Gross Rental Revenue Annual
Total income from nightly rental rates before any deductions. Does not include cleaning fees.
Formula
Gross Rental Revenue = Annual Nights × Nightly Rate
= (Nights/Month × 12) × Nightly Rate
In this app: grossRev = daysRented × nightlyRate — management fees are calculated on this figure only.
Cleaning Fee Revenue Annual
Revenue collected from guests to cover the cost of cleaning between stays. Treated as pass-through income — it is included in Total Revenue, reducing the break-even threshold. The management fee is not applied to cleaning revenue.
Formula
Cleaning Revenue = Stays/Month × 12 × Cleaning Fee per Stay
In this app: cleaningRev = staysPM × 12 × cleanFee. Default: 6 stays/month × $120/stay = $720/month.
Total Revenue Annual
All income the property generates in a year — the starting point for every expense and performance calculation.
Formula
Total Revenue = Gross Rental Revenue + Cleaning Revenue
In this app: totalRev = grossRev + cleaningRev
📋
Operating Expenses
Recurring costs of owning and operating the property
Property Tax Annual
A recurring tax levied by local government based on the assessed value of the property. Rates vary widely by jurisdiction — typically 0.5–2.5% of purchase price per year.
In this app: entered in Step 3. Auto-estimated at 1.2% of purchase price as a default if not manually provided. Included in both Total Expenses and NOI calculations.
Insurance Annual
Homeowner's or short-term rental insurance protecting the property against damage, liability, and loss of income. STR policies typically cost more than standard homeowner's policies due to increased guest traffic.
In this app: entered in Step 3. Auto-estimated at 0.5% of purchase price annually if not provided. Minimum default: $2,400/year.
HOA Fee Monthly
Mandatory fee paid to a Homeowners Association for shared amenities, maintenance, and community management. Applies to condos, townhouses, and planned communities. Entered monthly; converted to annual for calculations.
In this app: annualHOA = hoaMonthly × 12. Defaults to $0. If the AI auto-fills from a listing, this field is populated automatically.
Maintenance Monthly
Ongoing costs to keep the property in rentable condition — pool service, lawn care, HVAC servicing, minor repairs. A common rule of thumb is 1–2% of property value per year.
In this app: entered monthly; converted to annual. annualMaint = maintenance × 12. Default: $200/month.
Management Fee
A percentage-based fee paid to a property manager who handles bookings, guest communication, cleaning coordination, and maintenance. Applied only to rental revenue — not to cleaning fee revenue, which is a guest-to-host pass-through.
Formula
Management Fee = Gross Rental Revenue × Management Rate
Note: applied to rental revenue only — NOT to cleaning fees Management Rate = typically 20–25% for full-service STR management
In this app: mgmtFee = grossRev × mgmtPct. Default: 20%. Setting to 0% represents self-management.
Utilities & Supplies Fixed estimates
Utilities (electricity, water, internet, gas) and consumable supplies (toiletries, linens, paper goods) are fixed operating costs included automatically in every analysis.
Fixed Values Used in This App
Utilities = $300/month = $3,600/year
Supplies = $150/month = $1,800/year
In this app: editable monthly inputs (defaults: utilities = $300/mo, supplies = $150/mo), annualized as utilities = moUtilities × 12 and supplies = moSupplies × 12. Both are included in Total Expenses and NOI.
📊
Operating Performance Metrics
How the property performs as a business, independent of how it is financed
Net Operating Income (NOI)
Annual income after all operating expenses but before mortgage payments. NOI is the universally accepted measure of a property's standalone income-generating ability — it lets investors compare properties regardless of how they are financed. By definition, debt service is always excluded from NOI.
Formula
NOI = Total Revenue − Operating Expenses
= Total Revenue − Property Tax − Insurance − Management Fee
  − Utilities − Supplies − HOA − Maintenance
Mortgage is explicitly EXCLUDED — by industry definition, NOI is pre-financing. Operating Expenses = all costs except debt service
In this app: noi = totalRev − propTax − insurance − mgmtFee − utilities − supplies − annualHOA − annualMaint
Annual Cash Flow
The actual income remaining after all expenses including mortgage payments — the money an investor actually receives each year. Unlike NOI, this reflects the reality of being a leveraged investor who must service debt.
Formula
Annual Cash Flow = Total Revenue − Total Expenses
= NOI − Annual Mortgage Payments
Total Expenses = Mortgage + Property Tax + Insurance + Management Fee + Utilities + Supplies + HOA + Maintenance For all-cash purchases: Annual Cash Flow = NOI (no mortgage)
In this app: annualCF = totalRev − totalExp. This is the C used in the Gordon Growth Model valuation.
Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate) Display only
The annualized return a property would generate if purchased entirely in cash. Because it excludes financing, it is used to compare properties on a level playing field — independent of how any one investor chooses to fund the deal. A higher cap rate means more income relative to price.
Formula
Cap Rate = ( NOI ÷ Purchase Price ) × 100
NOI = Net Operating Income (pre-financing) Benchmark: STR properties typically target 6–10% cap rates
In this app: capRate = (noi / price) × 100. Shown for reference only — does not affect the investment verdict. The verdict is driven by NPV.
Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
Measures whether rental income can cover mortgage payments. Lenders require a DSCR of at least 1.25× for investment property loans — meaning rent must cover 125% of the mortgage. A DSCR below 1.0 means the property runs a cash deficit even before any other expenses.
Formula
DSCR = NOI ÷ Annual Mortgage Payment
DSCR ≥ 1.25 = Strong — lender-preferred threshold DSCR 1.00–1.25 = Marginal — income barely covers debt DSCR < 1.00 = Failing — rent does not cover the mortgage
In this app: dscr = noi / annualMtg. Displayed as N/A for all-cash purchases (no debt to cover).
Break-Even Occupancy
The minimum occupancy rate required for the property to generate positive cash flow. Below this threshold, the property loses money. Used as an operating health signal — not used in the investment verdict.
Formula
Break-Even Occ =
( Fixed Annual Expenses − Cleaning Revenue )
÷ ( 365 × Nightly Rate × (1 − Management Rate) ) × 100
Fixed Annual Expenses = Mortgage + Property Tax + Insurance + Utilities + Supplies + HOA + Maintenance Net revenue per night = Nightly Rate × (1 − Management Rate) — management fee reduces each dollar earned Cleaning Revenue is subtracted because it is collected regardless of nightly occupancy, reducing the threshold
In this app: beOcc = (fixedExpAnnual − cleaningRev) / (365 × nightlyRate × (1 − mgmtPct)) × 100

Investment Valuation — Gordon Growth Model
How the app converts rental income into a property value and an investment verdict
Discount Rate — Required Rate of Return (r)
The minimum annual return an investor demands to justify putting capital into this specific investment given its risk. Reflects the opportunity cost of capital — what you could earn in an alternative investment of similar risk (e.g. the S&P 500 historically returns ~10%/year; a riskier STR in a volatile market should require a higher hurdle).
Options in this App
6% — Low risk (stable rental market)
8% — Medium risk (typical Airbnb) ← default
10% — High risk (volatile STR market)
12% — Very high risk (speculative)
In this app: discount = riskLevelPct / 100. Selected in Step 4. Must strictly exceed the growth rate (r > g) for the valuation to be mathematically valid.
Income Growth Rate (g)
The expected rate at which annual rental income will grow perpetually into the future. Driven by rent inflation, market appreciation, and pricing power. A higher growth rate means the income stream is worth more today.
Options in this App
2% — Conservative ← default
4% — Moderate
6% — Aggressive
⚠ Constraint: r must strictly exceed g (r > g). If the growth rate equals or exceeds the discount rate, the perpetuity formula produces an infinite or nonsensical value. The app will show an error and withhold the verdict if this condition is violated.
In this app: g = growthPct / 100. Selected in Step 4.
Equity Value Gordon Growth Model
The present value of all future rental cash flows, assuming they grow at a constant rate forever. Based on the Gordon Growth Model (also called the Dividend Discount Model), originally developed to value stocks that pay growing dividends — applied here to rental income streams. The result tells you what the income stream is worth today given your required return.
Formula — Growing Perpetuity
Equity Value = C ÷ ( r − g )
C = Current annual cash flow (the exact figure shown in Operating Performance) r = Required rate of return (discount rate) g = Expected annual income growth rate r − g = The "spread" — smaller spread means higher value Important: C is NOT inflated by (1+g). Some textbooks use C₁ = C₀ × (1+g) when the next period's cash flow is known. Here we use the current observed cash flow directly — the growth assumption is already embedded in the (r−g) denominator, which prices in all future growth. Inflating C again would double-count the first year's growth.
In this app: incomeValue = annualCF / (discount − g)
For financed purchases: annualCF is the post-mortgage (levered) cash flow → Equity Value measures the return on equity deployed.
For all-cash purchases: annualCF is the unlevered cash flow → Equity Value measures the return on the full property value.
Net Present Value (NPV)
The difference between what the income stream is worth (Equity Value) and what you actually have to pay for it (Initial Investment). A positive NPV means you are acquiring a cash flow stream for less than its intrinsic value — the deal creates wealth. A negative NPV means you are overpaying relative to the income the property can generate.
Formula
NPV = Equity Value − Initial Investment
= C ÷ (r − g) − Initial Investment
Financed: Initial Investment = Down Payment + Closing Costs All-cash: Initial Investment = Purchase Price + Closing Costs NPV > 0 → income value exceeds capital deployed → wealth-creating deal NPV < 0 → income value is less than capital deployed → you are paying a premium over intrinsic value
In this app: npv = incomeValue − initialInvestment. NPV is the sole driver of the investment verdict (green / amber / red). Cap Rate and Cash-on-Cash are display-only.
🎯
Return Metrics
Additional performance measures displayed for context — not used in the verdict
Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC) Display only
Annual cash income expressed as a percentage of total cash invested. Measures the immediate yearly yield on deployed capital — analogous to a stock's dividend yield. A 10% CoC means you earn $10 in cash for every $100 invested, each year. Does not account for appreciation, loan paydown, or the time value of money.
Formula
Cash-on-Cash = ( Annual Cash Flow ÷ Initial Investment ) × 100
Annual Cash Flow = post-expense, post-mortgage income Initial Investment = down payment + closing costs (or full price for all-cash)
In this app: cashOnCash = (annualCF / initialInvestment) × 100. Shown as a reference metric — does not affect the verdict.
Payback Period
The number of years required to recover the initial investment entirely from operating cash flow. A simple, intuitive metric — but it ignores the time value of money (a dollar in year 10 is treated the same as a dollar in year 1). Shorter is better.
Formula
Payback Period = Initial Investment ÷ Annual Cash Flow
Returns "Not recovered" if Annual Cash Flow ≤ 0 (investment never pays back from operations alone)
In this app: paybackYrs = initialInvestment / annualCF when annualCF > 0, rounded to 1 decimal place.
⚖️
Investment Verdict Logic
How the app translates NPV into a buy / hold / pass recommendation
Verdict Thresholds
The verdict is determined exclusively by NPV — no other metric (cap rate, CoC, DSCR) affects the outcome. NPV is compared to zero and to a 30% loss threshold relative to initial investment.
Condition Rating Meaning
NPV > $0 ✅ Strong Investment Equity Value exceeds capital deployed. The income stream is worth more than you paid.
NPV > −30% of Initial Investment ⚠️ Marginal Income partially justifies the deal but falls short of recovering full capital.
NPV ≤ −30% of Initial Investment ❌ Challenging The income stream cannot justify this investment at current rates and assumptions.
Threshold Formulas
Green: NPV > 0
Amber: NPV > −( Initial Investment × 0.30 )
Red: NPV ≤ −( Initial Investment × 0.30 )
Display-only metrics: Cap Rate and Cash-on-Cash Return appear in the results for context but are explicitly excluded from the verdict calculation. The verdict is driven solely by NPV = C ÷ (r − g) − Initial Investment.
